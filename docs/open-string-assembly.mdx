After PCBA fabrication, OpenString is assembled by soldering the PCBs end-to-end and attaching the 3-wire harness. You can solder together as many boards as you like to create a string of any length. The assembly is then packaged for deployment using a combination of housing, potting, and sealing.

## Assembly Steps

The pages in this section walk through the full assembly workflow:

1. [Solder Boards](/docs/open-string-assembly-solder) — chain PCBs end-to-end and attach the 3-wire harness.
2. [Prepare Housing](/docs/open-string-assembly-housing) — example housing approach using extruded aluminum and 3D-printed end caps.
3. [Potting](/docs/open-string-potting) — prep the work surface and seal the assembly with marine epoxy.
4. [Testing & Mount](/docs/open-string-mount-validate) — run a final electrical check, then bond the string to its mounting surface (VHB tape) before deployment.

The packaging steps (housing, potting, VHB) necessarily depend on your specific application. We describe the SIMB3 approach as one concrete example. Adapt the materials and method to your own application.
