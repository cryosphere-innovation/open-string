## Parts, Materials, and Tools Needed

- [x] OpenString PCBs (any number, depending on the string length you need)
- [x] Housing (see [Prepare Housing](/docs/open-string-assembly-housing) for the SIMB3 example)
- [x] 3-conductor harness cable
- [x] Hookup wire
- [x] Soldering station (iron, solder, wetted sponge, etc.)
- [x] Box knife
- [x] Safety glasses, nitrile gloves

## Insert PCBs and Solder Together

Place the OpenString PCBs into the housing in the order they will sit in the finished string. Pay attention to orientation: the **bottom** of one board connects to the **top** of the next.

Connect the boards electrically by soldering each of the 4 castellated holes between adjacent boards. Reference the video for a visual guide.

![Soldering castellated joints](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/cryosphere-innovation/open-string/main/docs/images/temperature-string/12.jpeg "Each of the 4 castellated holes between adjacent boards should be soldered together to form a continuous measurement path.")

Make sure each castellated hole is soldered thoroughly, but be careful not to overheat the joint.

<Alert variant="success">
  <AlertDescription>
    When joining two boards, solder one castellated hole first. You can then reheat the joint to reposition the boards squarely before soldering the rest.
  </AlertDescription>
</Alert>

## Prepare the Harness Cable

The harness cable connects the string to your data logger or microcontroller.

1. Cut a section of harness cable to your desired length. For SIMB3, we cut it to 28 inches. 
2. Using a box knife, strip the outer insulation to leave an exposed section of insulated cable. The majority of the outer jacket should be removed.
3. Cut away the **green** wire, leaving only the red, black, and white conductors.
4. Strip the ends of the red, black, and white wires **1/4 inch**.
5. Tin each end. Leave a small ball of solder — it makes connecting the harness to the PCB easier later.

![Tinning harness wires](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/cryosphere-innovation/open-string/main/docs/images/temperature-string/11.jpeg "When tinning the three wires, leave a nice ball of solder. It makes the next step easier.")

## Insert Harness into Housing

Gently insert the harness through the hole in the top end of the housing. Only the insulated section should be exposed outside the housing.

## Solder the Harness to the Top PCB

Solder the three wires to the top PCB:

- **Red** → **VDD** castellated hole
- **Black** → **GND1** castellated hole
- **White** → **Data1** castellated hole

### Ground PIOA/B on the Top PCB

Using a stripped, tinned piece of hookup wire, connect the **PIOA/B** pad to the **GND1** pad on the top PCB. This is required for the chain's sequence-detection feature, which lets the firmware determine the position of each sensor at run-time.

![Harness connected to top PCB with PIOA/B grounded](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/cryosphere-innovation/open-string/main/docs/images/temperature-string/10.jpeg "Solder each of the three harness wires to the top PCB. Keep the wires flat and straight so they sit beneath the potting compound. For the PIOA/B → GND jumper, leaving a longer (1/2 inch) piece of hookup wire makes positioning easier.")

## Super Glue the Harness Wires to the Housing

1. Position the string and harness so the bottom edge of the first PCB is touching the bottom end of the housing. Place a dab of super glue on the bottom edge to hold it in place.

   ![Bottom PCB seated against housing end](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/cryosphere-innovation/open-string/main/docs/images/temperature-string/13.jpeg "If the bottom PCB isn't touching the bottom end piece, the 2 cm sensor spacing relative to the housing will be off.")

2. Starting near the top PCB, group the harness wires together and place a dab of super glue. Use accelerator to speed setting.
3. Repeat every 10 inches or wherever needed to keep the wires flat against the housing. Bulging wires will protrude through the potting compound.

## Seal the Top End with Super Glue

Apply a generous amount of super glue around the harness hole at the top end of the housing to seal it. Use accelerator to speed setting.

![Top end sealed with super glue](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/cryosphere-innovation/open-string/main/docs/images/temperature-string/14.jpeg "Sealing the harness hole prevents potting compound from leaking out during the next step.")

The string is now ready for [potting](/docs/open-string-potting), then [mounting and validation](/docs/open-string-mount-validate).
