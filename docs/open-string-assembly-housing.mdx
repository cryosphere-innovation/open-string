For most applications, OpenString PCBs will need to be secured in some kind of enclosure to protect them from the environment and allow physical mounting. 

The approach described here is the one used for SIMB3 and uses a piece of extruded plastic with 3D-printed end caps. The extrusion cradles the PCBs so they can be potted with epoxy and sealed for weather-proof use.

A DXF of the extrusion profile used for SIMB3 is [`available in the repository`](https://github.com/cryosphere-innovation/open-string/blob/main/hardware/solid-files/open-string-extrusion.DXF). You can use this file to source your own extrusion, or adapt the design to your needs. It is designed to be mounted externally using VHB tape to 4 inch diameter SCH 40 plumbing pipe.

We've also provided solid files for the top and center end caps used to seal the extrusion for potting — see the parts list below.

## Parts, Materials, and Tools Needed

- [x] Plastic extrusion of desired length
- [x] [`3D-printed top end cap`](https://github.com/cryosphere-innovation/open-string/blob/main/hardware/solid-files/open-string-top-end.stl)
- [x] [`3D-printed center end cap`](https://github.com/cryosphere-innovation/open-string/blob/main/hardware/solid-files/open-string-center-end.stl) 
- [x] Miter saw
- [x] Thick super glue + accelerator
- [x] Q-tips
- [x] Safety glasses, nitrile gloves, and ear protection

## Cut Extrusion to Length

Cut or assemble the extrusion to your target length. For reference: SIMB3 uses **78.5 inches**, which fits four PCBs (an 80-sensor string).

## Add the Top End Cap

Spread a layer of super glue into the recesses of the top end cap and slide it onto one end of the extrusion. Coat the corners thoroughly — that's where potting compound is most likely to leak out later.

![Top end glued and set](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/cryosphere-innovation/open-string/main/docs/images/temperature-string/7.jpeg "Left: top end cap freshly prepared with super glue. Right: top end cap fully set and bonded to the extrusion.")

## Add the Center End Cap

Repeat for the center end cap. If glue pools at the bottom, remove or spread the excess with a Q-tip.

![Center end glue cleanup](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/cryosphere-innovation/open-string/main/docs/images/temperature-string/6.jpeg "Note the buildup of super glue in the left picture. It must be removed or spread out so it doesn't interfere with seating the PCBs.")

## Seal the Backside

Flip the housing and apply a thin layer of super glue with a Q-tip along the seam between the extrusion and each end cap. Seal all visible gaps. Reference the video for technique.

![Backside seam sealed](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/cryosphere-innovation/open-string/main/docs/images/temperature-string/8.jpeg "The layer should be thick enough to seal visible gaps but thin enough not to protrude past the back surface of the housing.")

## Seal the Inner Side of the Top End

Flip the housing upright and apply another thin layer of super glue along the inner side of the top end.

![Inner top end sealed](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/cryosphere-innovation/open-string/main/docs/images/temperature-string/9.jpeg)

The housing is now ready for [board insertion and soldering](/docs/open-string-assembly-solder).
